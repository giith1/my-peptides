What Are Ipamorelin and CJC-1295 No-Dac?

Ipamorelin

Ipamorelin is a synthetic compound classified under growth hormone-releasing peptides (GHRPs). Structurally, it’s a pentapeptide—a chain of five amino acids. Its primary mode of action involves binding to the ghrelin receptor, which governs the release of growth hormone from the pituitary gland.

What is the TB500 & BPC157 Blend?

TB500 (Thymosin Beta-4 Fragment)

TB500 is recognized for its role in promoting cell growth, stimulating the formation of new blood vessels (angiogenesis), and supporting tissue repair. By interacting with actin, a protein crucial for cell structure and mobility, TB500 shows potential in enhancing the healing of soft tissues such as ligaments and tendons, particularly in injury recovery.

BPC157 (Body Protection Compound 157)

BPC157 is a synthetic peptide inspired by a natural protein known for protecting and healing the gastrointestinal (GI) tract. Research indicates its ability to enhance cell growth, boost collagen production, and facilitate blood vessel formation.

Research Benefits of Tesamorelin

  • Binding to GHRH Receptors
    Tesamorelin functions as a mimic of natural growth hormone-releasing hormone (GHRH).

  • Stimulating Growth Hormone Release
    By activating these receptors, Tesamorelin stimulates the anterior pituitary gland to produce and secrete growth hormone (GH).

  • Boosting IGF-1 Levels
    The rise in growth hormone levels prompts the liver to release insulin-like growth factor 1 (IGF-1).

  • Improving Lipid Metabolism
    Studies suggest that Tesamorelin may support the reduction of visceral fat by modulating lipid metabolism pathways.
